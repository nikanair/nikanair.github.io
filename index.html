~/research/interests

research/interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of sustainable computing, applied machine learning, and accessible environmental monitoring. I am particularly focused on developing innovative, deployable solutions that leverage machine learning and computer vision for real-world impact. One of my key research involves the Convolutional Neural Network based smartphone-based water quality monitoring system capable of detecting even low turbidity levels, making it suitable for applications such as drinking water analysis. This approach eliminates the need for expensive sensors or complex setups, supporting scalable, inclusive, and sustainable monitoring solutions, especially in resource-limited environments.


In parallel, I am deeply committed to promoting environmentally responsible AI practices. My work on the GreenAI application addresses the carbon footprint of machine learning models by quantifying emissions in real time and offering AI-driven optimization strategies to reduce energy consumption. Additionally, I explore computational efficiency through algorithmic optimization, such as enhancing factorial computations for better performance and lower memory usage. Across all my research, I strive to align technological innovation with environmental sustainability and practical utility.

initiatives

AIWQ IT Systems

Co-founder and CTO of a tech startup developing AI-powered solutions to monitor live water quality index using computer vision. We've secured US Embassy Green Economy Cohort training at Hyderabad, India and presently developing web based application for chemical, aqua farming and agricultural industries. We are deeply involved in developing hyperspectral image based water quality monitoring and WQI prediction without the use of expensive conventional sensors.

GAIN-AI.org

Founder of a non-profit that has empowered over 1000+ young students and professionals from across the globe with digital literacy, programming skills, cybersecurity and sustainable AI. Our initiative has now reached four continents, 10+ countries and attracting people from companies, colleges, schools and universities. GAIN at present has reached learners from Costco, Seneca, Michigan Technological University, Manchester Metropolitan University, York University, ISCTE Business School, The Lakefeild Schools, BigNet, Axis Colleges and many more.

BookLift

BookLift, I coded for a hackathon. Just thought of the idea that an app can collect used books almost non existent at remote schools and colleges, from someone just wanted to dumpt it or put it unopened in ashelf for decades. The code, did not win the hackathon, but won in collecting 1000+ text books and reference books in science and engineering over a period of 1 year. The total collected books market prize was beyond 10,00,000 INR. The books were then distributed to JDT, the reputed orphanage schools and colleges in Kerala, India. At present I am not running this app, but may defenitely bring it back in future.

LunchBell

LunchBell was the one I created for CS Base Hackathon. Lunchbell is built to solve the challenges of Mid Day Meal scheme for poor remote schools in India. It works as game-changer for farmers and poor schools to provide quality food at affordable rates. It was awarded with Wolfram Prize in Advanced Coding. The idea was to eliminate the wastage of food and later the app collected funds for meals for nearly 1200 school kids through Akshaya Patra Foundation, Bangalore, India. I will be reviving this project anytime soon to make it available for other foundations.
